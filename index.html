MMA

Mixed martial arts, often referred to by the English acronym MMA (mixed martial arts), and formerly called free combat or free-fight, is a complete combat sport, combining boxing and hand-to-hand combat. Both fighters can use many techniques; according to the federations, percussion techniques are authorized such as kicks, punches, knees and elbows, but also clinch, throwing and submission (grappling) techniques and sometimes specific techniques of ground impact.

Boxing

Boxing, also called the "sport of kings" or the noble art, is a sport in which two athletes of similar weight attack each other with their fists in a series of 1- to 3-minute intervals called "rounds". In both the Olympic and professional divisions, fighters (called boxers or wrestlers) dodge their opponents' punches while trying to land punches of their own. Points are awarded for clean, solid blows in the legal area on the front of the opponent's body above the waist, and blows to the head and torso are considered more valuable. The boxer with the most points after the specified number of rounds is declared the winner. Victory can also be achieved if the opponent is knocked down and unable to get up before the referee counts to ten (a knockout, or KO) or if the opponent is too injured to continue (a technical knockout, or TKO).

kickboxing

Kickboxing is a form of boxing that relies on kicking and punching and includes a group of mixed martial arts sports that include Japanese kickboxing, savat, sanda, Muay Thai and others. Kickboxing is practiced in a boxing ring using boxing gloves, mouth guards, shorts, and with bare feet to enhance the use of kicks. It is often practiced for self-defense, public fitness or competition
